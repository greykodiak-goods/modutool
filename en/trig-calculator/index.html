The trig ratios — SOH-CAH-TOA

In a right triangle, the three sides are named relative to one acute angle θ. The side across from θ is the opposite, the side touching θ is the adjacent, and the longest side, across from the right angle, is the hypotenuse. The trig ratios are simply ratios of these side lengths, and the classic mnemonic for them is SOH-CAH-TOA.

  • sin θ = opposite ÷ hypotenuse — SOH (Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse)
  • cos θ = adjacent ÷ hypotenuse — CAH (Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse)
  • tan θ = opposite ÷ adjacent — TOA (Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent)

The three values are not independent. The identity tan θ = sin θ ÷ cos θ always holds, and so does the Pythagorean identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1. Both are handy for checking answers you worked out by hand. The right triangle solver above uses exactly these ratios together with the Pythagorean theorem (a² + b² = c²) internally.

Degrees (°) ↔ radians (rad) conversion formula

Math, physics and engineering use both angle units, and the entire conversion rests on one fact: 180° = π rad.

  • Degrees → radians: rad = degrees × π ÷ 180 — e.g. 30° = 30 × π/180 = π/6 ≈ 0.5236 rad
  • Radians → degrees: degrees = rad × 180 ÷ π — e.g. 1 rad = 180/π ≈ 57.2958°

Whichever unit you type in, this calculator always shows both units on the first line of the result, so you never need a separate conversion table.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert between degrees (°) and radians (rad)?

The single conversion anchor is 180° = π rad. Multiply degrees by π/180 (about 0.017453) to get radians, and multiply radians by 180/π (about 57.2958) to get degrees. For example, 30° is π/6 ≈ 0.5236 rad. This calculator always shows both units on the first line of the result.

Why does tan 90° show as "Undefined"?

Because tan θ = sin θ ÷ cos θ, and cos 90° = 0 — you cannot divide by zero. At every angle where cos θ equals 0, such as 90° and 270°, the tangent is undefined, and this calculator displays "Undefined" instead of a value at those angles.

Which two values do I enter in the right triangle solver?

Any 2 of base a, height b, hypotenuse c and acute angle θ. The calculator detects which combination you entered and uses the Pythagorean theorem and trigonometric ratios to find the remaining values, plus the perimeter and area. Note that the hypotenuse c must be longer than either leg, and θ must be greater than 0° and less than 90°.

How accurate are the results?

Calculations use your browser's built-in double-precision (64-bit) floating-point arithmetic, accurate to roughly 15 significant digits, and results are displayed rounded to 6 decimal places. That is more than enough precision for checking homework and for practical engineering work.

Is my input sent to a server?

No. All calculations are done with JavaScript inside your browser, and your input is never transmitted anywhere. Once the page is open it keeps working even if your internet connection drops, and there is no sign-up or installation.
