Inclusive vs exclusive counting — when exactly is "day 100"?

The single biggest source of confusion in date math is whether or not the first day itself is counted. Standard D-day notation uses the plain date difference — a style widely used in Korea, where "D-7" simply means seven days before the target date. In other words, the N in D-N equals target date minus today; the target date itself is D-Day, and the days after it are D+1, D+2 and so on. If your exam is tomorrow, today is D-1, and exam day is D-Day.

Anniversaries follow a different convention. The day a relationship starts (or a baby is born) is counted as "day 1", which means the 100th day is the start date plus 99 days — inclusive counting. If a couple started dating on January 1, their 100th-day anniversary is April 10. Add a flat 100 days instead and you will celebrate a day late. As formulas: day number = elapsed days + 1, and the Nth day = start date + (N − 1) days. That is why this calculator has a "Count the first day as day 1" toggle to switch between the two methods — and when it is on, the standard D±N notation is shown alongside so nothing gets mixed up.

The same distinction matters beyond anniversaries. Contracts, visa windows, notice periods and statutory deadlines can be defined either exclusively (the first day is not counted, and the period starts from the next day) or inclusively (the first day counts as day 1) — and the two readings differ by exactly one day. The month conversion in this tool is calendar-based: one month is counted each time the same day of the following month is reached, which is more accurate than pretending every month has 30 days. Still, whenever a deadline has legal or financial consequences, double-check which counting rule applies before you rely on the number.

Frequently asked questions

What do D-1 and D+1 mean?

D-1 means one day before the target date (1 day left), the target date itself is D-Day, and D+1 means the day after the target date (1 day past). This calculator's default display follows this standard notation: the N in D-N is simply the date difference between the target date and today.

Why does a 100th-day anniversary fall on the start date plus 99 days?

By convention, anniversaries count the starting day itself as day 1, so the 100th day is the start date plus 99 days. In the third card, check "Count the first day as day 1" and press the 100-day preset, and the calculator applies this method automatically.

Do I get the same result no matter what time of day I calculate?

Yes. The calculator ignores the time of day and compares dates only, anchored to local midnight on your device, so the result is the same at any hour. If you are abroad, the local date set on your device is what counts.

How is the month conversion calculated?

It is calendar-based. One month is counted each time the same day of the following month is reached, and any leftover days are shown separately. This is the same calendar method commonly used for legal and contractual periods, and it is more accurate than treating every month as a flat 30 days.

Are the dates I enter stored or sent to a server?

No. All calculations run 100% inside your browser; your input is never sent to a server and is not stored anywhere. Refreshing the page resets everything.
