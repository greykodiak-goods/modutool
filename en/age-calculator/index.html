Why does Korea have three ages?

If you follow K-pop, K-dramas, or you live in Korea, you have probably noticed that a Korean person can give you two or even three different answers to "How old are you?" — and all of them are correct. Korea has historically used three parallel age systems:

  • International (Western) age (만나이, man-nai) — the age used almost everywhere else in the world. You are born at age 0 and gain a year on each birthday.
  • Korean calendar-year age (연나이, yeon-nai) — simply the current year minus the birth year. Everyone born in the same year "ages up" together on January 1, regardless of their birthday.
  • Traditional Korean age (세는나이, seneun-nai) — the famous "Korean age." You are 1 year old at birth, and everyone gains a year on New Year's Day. This is the system that can make a Korean person up to two years "older" than their Western age.

The 2023 age unification law

On June 28, 2023, Korea's so-called "age unification law" took effect. Formally, amendments to Article 7-2 of the General Act on Public Administration and Article 158 of the Civil Act made international (Western) age the default: unless a specific law says otherwise, ages in Korean statutes, contracts, and official documents are now calculated and stated as international age. The goal was to end the everyday confusion caused by three coexisting systems.

The underlying formula is simple:

  • Birthday already passed this year: age = reference year − birth year
  • Birthday not yet passed: age = reference year − birth year − 1

The tricky part is always "has the birthday passed as of the reference date?" — which this calculator determines automatically. All date math runs on your device's local midnight, so time-zone quirks never shift the result by a day.

Where calendar-year age still applies

The 2023 law did not convert everything. A few Korean laws deliberately keep calendar-year age (birth year only) for administrative simplicity — worth knowing if you live in Korea:

  • Youth Protection Act — buying alcohol and tobacco becomes legal from January 1 of the year a person turns 19 in international age, regardless of their actual birthday. This is why a convenience store clerk checks your birth year, not your birth date.
  • Military Service Act — draft physical examination notices go out by calendar-year age ("from January 1 of the year the person reaches that age").
  • Elementary and Secondary Education Act — children start elementary school in March of the year after the year they turn 6, so all children born in the same calendar year enter school together.

In short: for ID checks and military notices, look at the Korean calendar-year age shown by this calculator; for contracts and official paperwork, use the international age.

Frequently asked questions

How is international (Western) age calculated?

Subtract the birth year from the year of the reference date (usually today), then subtract one more if the birthday has not yet passed that year. For example, someone born in November 1990 has not had their birthday by July 2026, so 2026 − 1990 − 1 = 35. This calculator checks whether the birthday has passed automatically and applies the same rule.

What is Korea's 2023 age unification law?

It refers to amendments to Article 7-2 of the General Act on Public Administration and Article 158 of the Civil Act, passed by the Korean National Assembly in December 2022 and in force since June 28, 2023. Since that date, unless a specific law says otherwise, ages in Korean statutes, contracts, and official documents are calculated and stated as international (Western) age.

Are there cases where Korea still uses calendar-year age?

Yes. For administrative convenience, some laws still use calendar-year age (based only on the birth year): the Military Service Act for draft physical examination notices, the Youth Protection Act for the legal age to buy alcohol and tobacco, and the Elementary and Secondary Education Act for elementary school entry timing.

Is my date of birth sent to or stored on a server?

No. All calculations run as JavaScript inside your browser, and the date of birth you enter is never transmitted or saved anywhere. When you close the page, the input disappears with it.

How is the Chinese zodiac sign determined?

This calculator uses the solar (Gregorian) birth year to show one of the 12 zodiac animals (Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, Pig). In the traditional calendar the sign changes at Lunar New Year or the start of spring, so people born in January or early February may belong to the previous year's sign under the traditional rule.
