How to edit your PDF pages

  1. Drop in a PDF and every page appears as a preview thumbnail.
  2. Click ✕ to mark unwanted pages for deletion (click again to restore), use ◀ ▶ to move a page, and click ⟳ to rotate a crooked page in 90-degree steps.
  3. Click Save edited PDF and your edits are downloaded as a new file. The original stays untouched.

Why this tool?

Removing a page or two from a PDF is a simple job, yet most services make it a chore. Some require a login before you can edit anything, others cap you at a few uses per day, and desktop apps come bundled with ads. Worst of all, nearly every online editor uploads your file to the company's servers for processing.

ModuTool handles deleting, reordering, and rotating entirely inside your browser. Your file never leaves your device, so sensitive documents — contracts, medical records, ID scans — stay private. There's no upload wait, so even a document dozens of pages long finishes in seconds. No usage caps, no size limits, and no watermarks either.

Because you edit against live page previews, mistakes are much harder to make than with number-based tools. You can see exactly which page you're deleting, watch the new order take shape as you move pages around, and confirm a rotation fixed the orientation before you save anything.

💡 Tip: PDFs made with scanning apps often pick up a blank or blurry page in the middle. Delete those here, fix any upside-down pages with ⟳, and if you need to combine several files afterwards, finish up with Merge PDF.

When to use it

  • Submitting paperwork with only the pages that are needed — dropping a page with personal details before sending a job application or government form
  • Fixing a batch scan that came out shuffled by reordering pages against the previews
  • Rotating only the pages that were scanned sideways while leaving the rest alone
  • Deleting outdated pages from a report before replacing them with new ones

Good to know

This tool is built for page-level editing. If you want to extract a page range into a new file, Split PDF is faster; to save pages as images, use PDF to JPG. Password-protected PDFs cannot be edited for security reasons — remove the password first.

Frequently asked questions

Are my files really never uploaded to a server?

Correct. Deleting, reordering, and rotating all happen entirely inside your browser. Open your browser's network tab and you will see no file upload requests. Sensitive documents like contracts and ID scans are safe to use here.

Can I delete several pages at once?

Yes. Click the ✕ button on each page preview to mark as many pages as you like for deletion, then click Save once. Clicked the wrong one? The restore button in the same spot undoes it.

How do I reorder pages?

Use the ◀ ▶ arrow buttons on each page card to move it one position at a time. You work from the preview images, so there is no need to remember page numbers.

Can I rotate only the pages that were scanned sideways?

Yes. Rotation is applied per page. Click the ⟳ button on just the pages you want to turn — each click rotates that page 90 degrees while the rest stay untouched.

Is my original file modified?

No. The original PDF on your computer is left untouched — your edits are downloaded as a new file. If you don't like the result, just start again from the original.
