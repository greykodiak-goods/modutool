What is a pyeong? The formula and where it comes from

The pyeong (평, 坪) is Korea's traditional unit of floor area, and it is still the unit you will hear in every conversation about Korean apartments, offices and land — even though official paperwork switched to square meters years ago. If you are apartment-hunting in Seoul, dealing with a Korean landlord, or just reading Korean property listings, converting between pyeong and square meters is unavoidable.

The unit derives from the area of a square measuring 6 cheok (Korean traditional feet) on each side. One cheok is defined in metric terms as exactly 10/33 m (about 0.303 m), so 6 cheok is about 1.818 m. Squaring that gives (60/33)² = 400/121 m² = 3.30578512… m²; in practice everyone uses the six-decimal rounding, 1 pyeong = 3.305785 m², which works out to roughly 35.58 square feet. That is the constant this calculator uses. As a mental picture: one pyeong is about the space two adults need to lie down side by side.

  • m² = pyeong × 3.305785 (e.g. 25 pyeong × 3.305785 = 82.64 m²)
  • pyeong = m² × 0.3025 (1 m² = 121/400 pyeong = exactly 0.3025 pyeong; e.g. 84 m² × 0.3025 = 25.41 pyeong)

A bit of legal background helps too. Under Korea's metrology law, the square meter became the only legal unit of area from July 1, 2007. That is why real estate contracts, the property registry and apartment sales notices are written exclusively in m², while agents, landlords and neighbors still ask "how many pyeong is it?" out of habit. Two units, one market — hence the constant need to convert.

Exclusive area vs supply area — the trap for newcomers

This is the part of Korean real estate that confuses newcomers the most. The same apartment carries several very different area figures depending on what is being measured.

  • Exclusive area (전용면적, jeonyong myeonjeok) — the space inside your front door that only your household uses: rooms, living room, kitchen, bathrooms. Balconies are treated as bonus "service area" and are not included. This is the figure written in the property registry and your contract.
  • Supply area (공급면적, gonggeup myeonjeok) — the exclusive area plus your share of common residential spaces: stairways, corridors, elevator halls. The traditional "N-pyeong type" apartment labels are almost always based on this figure.
  • Contract area (계약면적, gyeyak myeonjeok) — the supply area plus the rest of the shared facilities: parking, management office, community rooms. It is mainly used for officetels (studio-type units), which is why an officetel of the same nominal "size" feels far smaller inside than an apartment.

The rule of thumb: however big the listing's headline number looks, compare homes by exclusive area — that is the space you actually live in. Punch the exclusive-area m² into this converter to get a feel for it in pyeong, the unit every Korean around you will be using.

Frequently asked questions

Exactly how many square meters is 1 pyeong?

1 pyeong is 3.305785 m² — precisely 400/121 m² (= 3.30578512… m²), or about 35.58 square feet. The unit comes from the area of a square measuring 6 cheok (traditional Korean feet, about 1.818 m) on each side. Going the other way, 1 m² is exactly 0.3025 pyeong.

How many pyeong is an 84 m² apartment, and why is it called a "34-pyeong type"?

An exclusive area of 84 m² is about 25.4 pyeong. The market nickname "34-pyeong type" exists because the traditional size label is based on the supply area (roughly 110 m²), which adds shared spaces such as stairways and corridors on top of the exclusive area. That difference in the reference area is why multiplying the exclusive-area figure by 0.3025 does not match the nickname.

Why do Korean contracts and registries use m² instead of pyeong?

Under Korea's metrology law, the square meter (m²) became the only legal unit of area from July 2007. Official documents — real estate contracts, property registries, apartment sales notices — use m² exclusively, while pyeong lives on as the customary unit in everyday conversation and listings.

What is the difference between exclusive area, supply area and contract area?

Exclusive area is the space inside your front door used only by your household (rooms, living room, kitchen, bathrooms). Supply area adds shared residential spaces such as stairways, corridors and elevator halls. Contract area adds the rest of the shared facilities on top — parking, management office and so on. The traditional Korean apartment size nicknames are usually based on the supply area.
