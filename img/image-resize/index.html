    What Makes This Different from Other Resizing Sites

    Most online image tools upload your photos to their own servers, process them there, and send them back. You have no way of knowing when the uploaded copies are deleted or who can see them along the way. For sensitive images — pictures of your kids, ID scans, contract documents — that is an uncomfortable thought.

    ThisIsMyIMG's image resizer never sends your files anywhere. The entire resize and conversion runs inside your computer (or phone) using the canvas technology built into your browser. That design brings three advantages.

    • Privacy — your photos never leave your device, so there is simply nothing to leak.
    • Speed — with no upload or download waits, even dozens of photos finish in seconds.
    • No limits — since we pay no server costs, there are no caps on file count, runs, or size.

    The quality handling is different, too. If you shrink a 4,000px photo straight down to 800px in one step, the browser's default resampling tends to produce shimmering, jagged edges on fine lines. This tool uses progressive downscaling — halving the image in several steps before landing on the target size — so even drastic reductions keep fine details like text, hair, and grid patterns sharp. It also automatically applies the EXIF orientation data in smartphone photos taken in portrait mode, so you'll never hit the classic problem other tools have where "the converted photo comes out sideways."

    Format conversion comes up more often than you might think. A photo that doesn't need a transparent background is just wasted bytes as a PNG — converting it to JPG or WEBP is the better choice — while logos and icons that do need transparency should stay PNG or WEBP. Because this one tool handles both resizing and format conversion, you won't have to bounce between multiple sites uploading and re-downloading the same photos.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Are my photos really never uploaded to a server?

    Correct. Resizing and format conversion happen entirely on your device using the canvas feature built into your browser. Open your browser's developer tools and check the Network tab — you won't see a single file upload request. Sensitive images like family photos or ID scans are safe to use here.

    Will downsizing ruin the image quality?

    Shrinking a large image in one big step tends to create jagged, aliased edges. This tool uses progressive downscaling — halving the image in several steps before landing on the final size — to preserve as much sharpness as possible. For JPG and WEBP, quality around 85 shows virtually no visible degradation.

    What do I gain by converting to WEBP?

    WEBP is a modern image format that typically saves 25–35% compared to JPG at the same visual quality. Using it on blogs, online stores, and websites speeds up page loading, and it opens in every modern browser including Chrome, Edge, Safari, and Samsung Internet. Like PNG, it also supports transparent backgrounds.

    Can iPhone HEIC photos be converted?

    Most browsers can't read HEIC directly, so those files may be skipped during conversion. On your iPhone, go to Settings → Camera → Formats and choose 'Most Compatible' to shoot in JPG, or share the photo as a JPG from the Photos app and it will work right away.

    Will my portrait photos come out sideways?

    The tool automatically applies the EXIF orientation data stored in smartphone photos, so after conversion your images are saved facing exactly the way you originally saw them. You don't need to worry about photos coming out rotated or flipped.
