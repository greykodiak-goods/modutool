What the values mean

Every pixel on screen is a mix of red, green, and blue light, each from 0 to 255. RGB notation lists them directly — rgb(37, 99, 235) — while HEX packs the same three numbers into base-16: #2563EB. They are interchangeable; HEX is simply the form most tools and style guides expect, so that is what the copy buttons produce.

One honest caveat: browser canvases read colors in sRGB. A photo saved in a wider gamut (Display P3 is common on recent phones) is converted to sRGB first, so very saturated colors can read a few steps off from what a color-managed editor like Photoshop reports. For ordinary web graphics and screenshots the values match exactly.

Getting a precise pick

  • Zoom in — browser zoom (Ctrl/Cmd +) or pinch-zoom on mobile. The eyedropper follows the zoomed image, so single-pixel details become easy targets.
  • Pick from flat areas — JPG compression blends pixels at edges, so a pixel on the boundary of text or a border reads as a mix. The middle of a solid region gives the true value.
  • Prefer PNG screenshots — PNG is lossless, so UI colors survive exactly. JPG screenshots can shift flat colors by a point or two.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my image uploaded anywhere?

No. The image is drawn onto a canvas inside your browser and colors are read from that canvas on your device. You can open the Network tab of your browser's developer tools and confirm no file is sent anywhere. Closing the tab removes everything.

Why does the HEX differ slightly from what Photoshop or my design file shows?

Browser canvases work in sRGB. If your photo was saved in a wider color space such as Display P3 (common on iPhones), the browser converts it to sRGB before we read the pixel, so saturated colors can come out a few steps different from what a color-managed editor reports. For pixels that were already sRGB — most web graphics and screenshots — the values match exactly.

How do I pick exactly the right pixel?

Zoom in: use your browser's zoom (Ctrl/Cmd +) on desktop or pinch-zoom on mobile — the readout follows the zoomed pixels, so you can land on a 1-pixel detail. The floating chip shows the color under the cursor before you click, so watch it stabilize before locking a pick.

What's the difference between HEX and RGB?

They are two notations for the same color. RGB lists the red, green, and blue components as numbers from 0 to 255, e.g. rgb(37, 99, 235). HEX writes those same three numbers in base-16 as one string, #2563EB. CSS accepts both; design tools and brand guidelines usually quote HEX, which is why the copy buttons here copy the HEX form.

Why does a color from my screenshot not match the website's CSS?

Screenshots saved as JPG blur colors slightly at edges — compression mixes neighboring pixels, so a pixel at the boundary of text or a button border can read as a blend. Pick from the middle of a flat color area and use a PNG screenshot when you can; PNG is lossless, so flat areas keep their exact values.
