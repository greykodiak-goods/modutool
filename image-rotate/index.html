    What Makes This Different from Other Rotate Sites

    Most online image tools upload your photos to their own servers, process them there, and send them back. You have no way of knowing when the uploaded copies are deleted or who can see them along the way. For sensitive images — pictures of your kids, ID scans, contract documents — that is an uncomfortable thought.

    ThisIsMyPDF's rotate & flip tool never sends your files anywhere. The entire operation runs inside your computer (or phone) using the canvas technology built into your browser. That design brings three advantages.

    • Privacy — your photos never leave your device, so there is simply nothing to leak.
    • Speed — with no upload or download waits, even dozens of photos finish in seconds.
    • No limits — since we pay no server costs, there are no caps on file count, runs, or size.

    Rotation here is lossless in every way that matters. Turning an image 90°, 180°, or 270° and flipping it only rearranges pixels that already exist — nothing is scaled or resampled, so lines, text, and fine detail stay exactly as sharp as the original. When a 90° or 270° turn is applied the canvas swaps its width and height automatically, so a portrait photo becomes a proper landscape without any cropping or black bars. PNG files come out pixel-for-pixel identical, and JPG and WEBP are re-encoded a single time at high quality, which is visually indistinguishable from the source.

    The tool also reads the EXIF orientation data that phone cameras write into portrait shots, so images open the right way up before you touch anything — no more guessing whether a photo is "really" sideways or just tagged that way. And because the format is preserved, a JPG stays a JPG and a transparent PNG keeps its transparency; you are only changing the direction the picture faces, nothing else.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Are my photos really never uploaded to a server?

    Correct. Rotating and flipping happen entirely on your device using the canvas feature built into your browser. Open your browser's developer tools and check the Network tab — you won't see a single file upload request. Sensitive images like family photos or ID scans are safe to use here.

    Does rotating an image reduce its quality?

    Rotating by 90°, 180°, or 270° and flipping only rearrange the existing pixels — there is no resizing or resampling, so no detail is lost. JPG and WEBP files are re-encoded once when saved, which is visually indistinguishable from the original, while PNG stays pixel-for-pixel identical.

    My photo was taken sideways and shows up rotated. How do I fix it?

    Load the photo and tap Rotate Left 90° or Rotate Right 90° until it stands upright, then download. The tool also reads the EXIF orientation stored by phone cameras, so most portrait shots already appear correctly and just need a quick nudge if they don't.

    What is the difference between rotate and flip?

    Rotate turns the whole image around its center in 90° steps. Flip mirrors it — horizontal flip swaps left and right (perfect for undoing the mirror effect on front-camera selfies so text reads correctly), and vertical flip swaps top and bottom. You can combine rotation and flips, and the preview shows the current state.

    Does it keep the original file format?

    Yes. A JPG is saved as JPG, a PNG as PNG, and a WEBP as WEBP, so the file behaves exactly as before — only its orientation changes. GIF and BMP files, which the browser cannot re-encode, are saved as PNG instead.
