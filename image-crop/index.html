How to Use

  1. Drag an image into the area above, or click it to select one. JPG, PNG, WEBP, GIF, and BMP are supported — one image at a time.
  2. Optionally pick an aspect ratio. Leave it on Free to crop any shape, or choose 1:1, 4:3, 16:9, or 3:4 to lock the selection to that proportion.
  3. Drag across the preview to draw a crop box, then drag inside the box to reposition it until it frames exactly what you want.
  4. Click Crop Image. The result shows the original and cropped resolution, and the file downloads automatically as name_cropped.

What Makes This Crop Tool Different

Most online image tools upload your photo to their own servers, crop it there, and send it back. You have no way of knowing when the uploaded copy is deleted or who can see it along the way. For sensitive images — ID scans, screenshots of documents, private pictures — that is an uncomfortable thought.

ThisIsMyPDF's cropper never sends your file anywhere. The whole crop runs inside your computer or phone using the canvas technology built into your browser. That design brings three advantages.

  • Privacy — your photo never leaves your device, so there is simply nothing to leak.
  • Speed — with no upload or download waits, the crop is instant.
  • No limits — since we pay no server costs, there are no caps on how many images you crop or how large they are.

Cropping here also stays pixel-perfect. The tool keeps the pixels inside your selection at their original resolution — it never stretches or resamples them — so the part you cut out is exactly as sharp as the source. It reads the EXIF orientation baked into smartphone photos first, so a picture taken in portrait mode shows the right way up before you even start, and it keeps the original format (JPG stays JPG; PNG and WEBP are saved as PNG so transparency survives).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my photo really never uploaded to a server?

Correct. Cropping happens entirely on your device using the canvas feature built into your browser. Open your browser's developer tools and check the Network tab — you won't see a single file upload request. Sensitive images like ID scans or private photos are safe to use here.

How do I crop to an exact aspect ratio like 1:1?

Pick a ratio from the aspect-ratio menu — Free, 1:1 (square), 4:3, 16:9, or 3:4 (portrait). Once a ratio is locked, the selection box keeps that shape no matter how you drag, so you get a perfectly square profile picture or a widescreen thumbnail without measuring anything by hand.

Does the output keep the original image format?

Yes. A JPG stays a JPG, while PNG and WEBP files are saved as PNG so any transparent background is preserved. The downloaded file is named after your original with a _cropped suffix added, so you can tell the two apart at a glance.

Will cropping reduce the image quality?

No. Cropping simply keeps the pixels inside your selection at their original resolution — it does not stretch or resample them — so the cropped area is exactly as sharp as it was in the source. The result panel shows both the original and the cropped resolution so you know the final pixel size.

Will my portrait photos come out sideways?

The tool automatically applies the EXIF orientation data stored in smartphone photos, so the image you crop is already facing the right way and the saved file keeps that orientation. You don't need to worry about photos coming out rotated or flipped.
