The factors and formulas behind the results

Wherever an exact legal definition exists, this converter uses it: 1 inch = 25.4 mm, 1 pound = 453.59237 g, 1 US gallon = 3.785412 L, 1 pyeong = 3.305785 m² (400/121 m², the Korean real-estate area unit — see the dedicated pyeong calculator for apartment-size context). Every conversion goes through the category's base unit (meters, kilograms, square meters, liters), so any pair of units in a category converts consistently.

Temperature is the exception — the scales start at different zero points, so a factor alone can't do it. The converter applies the exact formulas:

  • °F = °C × 9/5 + 32 (and back: °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9)
  • K = °C + 273.15 — the Kelvin scale starts at absolute zero, −273.15 °C

That zero-point offset is why "double the temperature" makes no sense across scales, and why 0 °C is 32 °F, not 0 °F.

Watch out for "same name, different unit"

Several everyday units have regional variants, and this tool picks one deliberately:

  • Gallon — this converter uses the US liquid gallon (3.785412 L). The UK imperial gallon is 4.54609 L, about 20% bigger.
  • Cup — recipes disagree: US legal cup 240 ml (used here), US customary 236.6 ml, metric cup 250 ml, Japanese cup 200 ml. For baking, a few ml rarely matters; for precise work, weigh instead.
  • Ton — here, the metric ton (1,000 kg). The US short ton (907.2 kg) and UK long ton (1,016 kg) are different animals.

Internal math runs at full floating-point precision and only the display is rounded — to at most 6 significant digits, with trailing zeros trimmed. That is more than enough for cooking, travel, real estate and homework. If you are doing engineering, trade or legal work, use the exact defined factors above and apply the rounding rules your field requires.

Frequently asked questions

What conversion factor is used for the Korean pyeong?

1 pyeong = 3.305785 m² (precisely 400/121 m²), the standard factor used in Korean real estate, and going the other way 1 m² = 0.3025 pyeong exactly. The pyeong derives from a square measuring 6 cheok (traditional Korean feet, about 1.818 m) per side and is still the everyday unit for Korean apartments and land, even though official documents use square meters.

What formulas are used for temperature conversion?

Temperature is not a simple multiplication because the scales have different zero points, so exact formulas are used: °F = °C × 9/5 + 32, °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9, K = °C + 273.15. For example 100 °C = 212 °F = 373.15 K. That offset is also why a 1-degree step means the same in Celsius and Kelvin but only 5/9 as much in Fahrenheit.

What is the difference between metric and imperial (US customary) units?

Metric units (mm, m, km, g, kg, L) scale by powers of ten, while imperial and US customary units (inches, feet, miles, ounces, pounds, gallons) use historical ratios like 12 inches to a foot. The bridges are exact definitions: 1 inch = 25.4 mm, 1 pound = 453.59237 g. Note that this tool uses the US liquid gallon (3.785412 L) and a 240 ml cup; UK imperial gallons (4.546 L) and metric 250 ml cups differ.

How precise are the results?

Conversion factors are exact where an exact definition exists (e.g. 1 in = 25.4 mm, 1 lb = 0.45359237 kg) and results are displayed to at most 6 significant digits with trailing zeros trimmed. Internal math runs at full floating-point precision, so rounding only happens at display time. For engineering or legal purposes, apply your own rounding rules to the raw factors.

Is the value I enter sent to a server?

No. All conversions run inside your browser using built-in constants and formulas — no network request is made, nothing is stored, and the page keeps working offline once it has loaded.
